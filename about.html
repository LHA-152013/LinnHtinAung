The longsword is a type of European sword characterized by a cruciform hilt with a grip designed for one or two hands, typically 15 to 30 cm (6 to 12 inches) long, and a straight double-edged blade measuring around 80 to 110 cm (31 to 43 inches) in length, weighing approximately 2 to 3 kg (4 lb 7 oz to 6 lb 10 oz) Wikipedia Wikipedia . It was widely used during the late medieval and Renaissance periods, roughly from 1350 to 1550 Wikipedia Wikipedia +1 . The weapon is also known as a bastard sword or hand-and-a-half sword, reflecting its versatility in grip and use.
The rapier originated in Spain as the espada ropera (“dress sword”) and in Italy as the spada da lato, first appearing in the mid-15th century Wikipedia Wikipedia +1 . It became widely popular across Western Europe during the 16th and 17th centuries, serving as both a status symbol for nobility and a practical weapon for dueling, self-defense, and military sidearms Wikipedia Wikipedia +1 . Its development was influenced by the decline of heavy armor and the Renaissance emphasis on elegance, speed, and individual skill.
